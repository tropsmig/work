The HODUCLICK tablet version project is designed for meeting room reservations, allowing users to book via the web and app, then use the tablet physically at the meeting room entrance. The tablet version was requested in English and features three modes: OCCUPIED (in use), WAITING (pre-check-in), and AVAILABLE (available for booking). The design uses color coding for intuitive user recognition.

The tablet version was requested before the web version, and due to its specific usage purpose, it was planned and designed differently from the web version. To enhance user convenience, the main color of HODUCLICK was excluded. The design uses three modes with traffic light-inspired colors, ensuring that both HODUCLICK members and general users can use the app without separate training.

Guidelines

The key focus was to create an app that everyone could use without separate training, so the colors were inspired by the familiar traffic light, ensuring ease of use for all users.

  • image of three circles of each color

    • Red OCCUPIED (In use) The first mode uses red to signal that entry is prohibited, with reduced saturation to minimize eye strain during prolonged use.

    • Yellow WAITING (Pre-check-in) The second mode, yellow, signals that check-in is required upon arrival. The yellow color is also muted to suit the usage context.

    • Green AVAILABLE (Available for booking) The third mode uses bright green to indicate room availability, adding vibrancy to the design, as it's controlled through the web and app.

WAITING

This is the pre-check-in mode, displayed when users arrive at the meeting room at their reserved time. Check-in must be completed within 10 minutes of the reservation start time, or the booking will be canceled. It’s the intermediate mode, selected in yellow.

When You Arrive

By clicking the check-in button, users can immediately use the reserved meeting room. After check-in, the mode changes to OCCUPIED (in use).

OCCUPIED

This mode indicates "currently unavailable" after check-in, controlling access and showing available reservation times. Red was chosen to minimize eye strain while maintaining design vibrancy.

When The Room is In Use

Red, the best color to indicate "unavailable," was chosen. Only authorized users can end the meeting early.

AVAILABLE

Green was chosen to indicate availability for booking. Although the tablet version was initially planned to support bookings, it was later limited to web and app, leading to a design adjustment. A different color might have been chosen if known earlier.
