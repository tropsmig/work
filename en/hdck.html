The meeting room reservation app is split into a tablet version for on-site use and a web/app version for both meeting room reservations and management. Through the web/app version, users can check the availability of meeting rooms, their capacity, and available equipment (such as projectors, Wi-Fi, etc.), as well as make reservations. The tablet version is designed to allow both registered and non-registered users to access the system. (For more details, please refer to the case study via the top project.)

Based on the existing web version, everything except for the logo, main color, and equipment icons was re-planned, redesigned, and developed. The request to "view a lot of information at a glance without multiple clicks" was incorporated as much as possible during the creation process.

Design Guidelines

This app includes five menus: Meeting Room Management, Status, and Settings. All design and development were independently created, created, except for the logo, main color, and equipment icons.

  • Main Color #2295F3

What I Considered

This app is designed for use in large companies, with user preferences reflected based on gender and age. It is expected that more men will use the app, with the highest usage in the 40s age group. A clean and intuitive design with broad appeal was prioritized, and to add some fun to the design, avatars were incorporated to make the interface more engaging.

Reserve
a Meeting Room.

The main screen features a mini calendar and a left-side menu, with the main content displaying a list of registered meeting rooms. Each room shows a photo, basic information, and a "reservation bar" that indicates the booking status, with colors to differentiate availability.

image of a web version of calendar

  • Side Menu
    Below the mini calendar on the left, the display shows the reservation list for regular users, while administrators can also view the reservation approval list.
    image of left side menu
  • Reservation Status UI
    The "reservation bar" uses color coding to indicate availability: light gray means unavailable, white and light blue mean available, blue indicates fully booked, and yellow shows the user's own reservation.
    • Not Available
    • Available to Book
    • Your Reservation
    • Nearly Full
    • Full
  • Reservation Modal
    Reservations are made via a modal without screen transitions. Key meeting room info (equipment icons, capacity) appears at the top, with reservation and cancel buttons on the right. The intuitive design requires no training.
    image of reservaton modal
